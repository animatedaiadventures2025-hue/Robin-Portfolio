🚨 FAILURE POINTS & HOW TO AVOID THEM

1. Quality Control Failures

Risk: Receiving defective/stained items, wrong sizes, color mismatch, poor stitching
Impact: Returns, negative reviews, loss of customer trust, wasted shipping costs
✅ Prevention:
  • Order samples FIRST (₹500-1000 per fabric) before bulk
  • Request video QC from supplier for every batch
  • 10% extra units as buffer for defects
  • Create detailed QC checklist: fabric, stitching, sizing, color, packaging
  • Have backup suppliers ready (2-3 on standby)

2. Cash Flow Crises

Risk: Running out of money before break-even, unable to reorder, can't pay suppliers
Impact: Business shutdown, stuck inventory
✅ Prevention:
  • Keep 30% of budget as reserve (~$600)
  • Don't spend on non-essentials until Month 4
  • Negotiate payment terms with suppliers (30% advance, 70% on delivery)
  • Start with just 100-150 units, not 200
  • Track weekly expenses meticulously

3. Supplier Scams / Unreliable Partners

Risk: Supplier takes payment and doesn't deliver, delivers wrong product, disappears
Impact: Total loss of inventory investment
✅ Prevention:
  • Never pay 100% upfront - max 30-50%
  • Use escrow/Trade Assurance on Alibaba/IndiaMART
  • Verify company registration (GST number)
  • Start with small order (50 units) as test
  • Get references from existing clients
  • Visit factory in person if possible

4. Marketing Budget Burn

Risk: Spending too much on ads with no returns, targeting wrong audience
Impact: $500-1000 wasted in a week
✅ Prevention:
  • Start with $5/day ad budget, not $20
  • Test one ad creative per week
  • Use Instagram for organic reach first
  • Create lookalike audiences from email list
  • Retarget website visitors (pixel)
  • Set strict ROAS threshold: minimum 2x before scaling

5. Platform / Tech Failures

Risk: Shopify down, payment issues, domain expiry, site hacked
Impact: Lost sales, customer data breach
✅ Prevention:
  • Enable 2FA on all accounts
  • Keep Shopify theme simple (avoid custom code)
  • Use Shopify Payments as primary (automatic failover)
  • Set calendar reminders for domain renewal
  • Backup store data weekly

    • 6. Shipping / Fulfillment Nightmares

    Risk: Lost packages, delayed shipments, customs issues, wrong addresses
    Impact: Customer complaints, refunds, negative reviews
    ✅ Prevention:
    • Use ShipStation or similar for label generation
    • Require address confirmation before shipping
    • Buy shipping insurance for orders >$50
    • Keep digital copy of customs docs
    • Use poly mailers with inner bags for protection
    • Set shipping expectations on product page

    7. Launch Timing Mistakes

    Risk: Launching during wrong season, major competition (Black Friday), religious holidays
    Impact: Low initial sales, missed opportunity
    ✅ Best Launch Time:
    • Best: January (New Year, winter clearance) OR March (pre-Ramadan)
    • Avoid: December (Black Friday dominates) OR June-August (back to school)
    • Ramadan Window: Launch 4-6 weeks BEFORE Ramadan starts

    WEEK 4 (Days 22-28)
    🇮🇳 India: Receive samples, QC them, select primary supplier, negotiate final prices
    🇺🇸 USA: Launch Instagram page, start hashtag research, create email capture, setup pixel
    WEEK 5 (Days 29-35)
    🇮🇳 India: Place order for 100 units (50% payment), start production
    🇺🇸 USA: Soft launch to friends/family (10-20 people), gather feedback, fix issues
    WEEK 6 (Days 36-42)
    🇮🇳 India: Production in progress, QC during production, arrange shipping
    🇺🇸 USA: Create first 3 Instagram ads ($5/day test), reply to all feedback, gather emails
    WEEK 7 (Days 43-49)
    🇮🇳 India: QC complete, pack inventory, ship to USA (DHL/FedEx), send tracking
    🇺🇸 USA: Prepare for launch, setup email welcome series, create launch post
    WEEK 8 (Days 50-56)
    🇮🇳 India: Track shipment, resolve any issues, prepare next reorder
    🇺🇸 USA: 🚀 OFFICIAL LAUNCH! - Post on all social, run ads $15/day, email list, offer discount code
    ⏰ AGGRESSIVE BUT REALISTIC: 8 weeks from start to launch is fast but doable. Key is supplier already selected by Week 4 so production can start Week 5.

    🏠 SIDE HUSTLE VS BUSINESS

    Side Hustle Phase

    WhenWhatBenefit
    Months 1-6Single member, no formal businessNo filing fees, simple taxes
    Revenue <$10KUse personal bank accountEasy bookkeeping
    Part-time effortEvening/weekend onlyNo risk to main income

    Transition to Business

    ⚠️ Make this switch when:
    • Revenue exceeds $1,000/month consistently (3+ months)
    • You need to hire help (VA, fulfillment)
    • You want to sell on Amazon/other platforms (require business license)
    • You need business credit/payment terms from suppliers
    • Taxes become too complex (multiple states)
    • Liability risk increases (defective products)

    Recommended Timeline

    PhaseTimelineStructure
    TestMonths 1-3Side hustle (sole prop)
    ValidateMonths 4-6Keep side hustle OR form LLC
    ScaleMonth 7+Form LLC if revenue justifies
    💡 KEY: For first $5-10K, stay as side hustle. LLC only makes sense when profit is $2000+/month to justify $150-300/yr in fees.
